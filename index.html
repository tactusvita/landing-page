HOW IT WORKS

Our models think with you, so that you can make better-informed decisions

Create a new patient profile

Before getting any therapy recommendations, we need to know your patient.

You start by creating a new patient profile where you input crucial information such as demographics, biomarkers, and previously diagnosed pathologies. Additionally, we support a wide array of medical files (e.g., MRI images, CT scans, radiographies) and single-cell analysis result formats.

Finally, we engage our AI models to provide a list of therapies ranked by expected success rate based on the unique circumstances of your patient.

Launch a therapy simulation

Once we have a better vision of your patient, you unlock the true potential of our platform: therapy simulations.

You launch a simulation by accessing your patient profile from the patient hub. Here, you can choose from a range of supported therapies and filter by category (e.g., surgery, chemotherapy, immunotherapy, radiation).

After selecting your therapy, you configure several parameters depending on the category. For example, you may want to experiment with adjusting radiation dosage, lowering it from an ongoing treatment to predict whether the patient will respond positively.

Interpret the results

As soon as you submit your simulation, we will redirect you to your simulation's dashboard.

Some of the elements we currently support are: 3D digital organ twin, interactive gene network mapping, heterogeneous multi-omic graph explorer, and more that are coming soon!

The power of running these simulations is that you are free to re-launch them at will and explore the patient's evolution across their pathology timeline. They will be all tracked and stored under your patient's profile, accessible from the patient hub.

Share your insights

The insights you can extract from our platform don't stay only with you.

We believe in the power of collaborative research, so we provide you with a range of export tools such as automatic report generation and simulation dashboard sharing.

ML-POWERED MODELS

Our models are fine-tuned for cellular insights and optimized therapy selection

UNA: Cellular Navigator

UNA serves as a navigator, delving into the intricate landscapes within a patient's cells. It maps gene interactions, regulatory regions, and cellular behaviors to uncover patterns that define health and disease. By creating this comprehensive map, UNA identifies the most promising targets for therapy, laying the foundation for precision medicine.

TSAR: Therapy Strategist

TSAR is the strategist, piecing together the complex puzzle of a patient's condition. It evaluates and ranks therapies by analyzing the connections between treatments, genes, and the patient's biological profile. TSAR's approach ensures the selection of optimal treatments, balancing effectiveness with minimal side effects for the best possible outcomes.
